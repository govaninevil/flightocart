History of United Airlines


United Airlines is one of the world’s most recognizable airlines. The airline has been in operation since 1927 and has grown to be one of the largest airlines in the world. United Airlines traces its roots back to two separate predecessor airlines - De Havilland Aircraft Company and Transcontinental & Western Air Lines. United Airlines was founded on April 12, 1927, when Frank A. Maurice decided to merge his two airlines. The early years for United were tough, but the airline continued to grow and by the early 1950s, it had become one of the largest carriers in the world. In 1952, United started serving international flights and within a few years, it became one of the leading international airline providers. In 1978, United merged with Ted Stevens' Alaska Airlines to form what is now United Continental Holdings Incorporated. Over the years, United has made significant updates to its fleet and operations. In 2007, the airline announced plans to fly from Washington Dulles International Airport using new Boeing 737-800 aircraft by 2009. The new planes were eventually delivered in 2010 and have drastically improved passenger service at Dulles Airport. In 2012, United announced plans to invest more than $5 billion over five years into its network starting with new aircraft orders and projects like adding more capacity on existing routes. The investment has helped make United one of the fastest-growing airlines in terms of passenger traffic globally.

United Airlines Cancellation Policy


This blog article discusses United Airlines cancellation policy. Cancellations made more than 24 hours before the flight is subject to a re-booking fee of $100 per person. Cancellations made within 24 hours of the scheduled departure time are not subject to a re-booking fee, but they may result in a loss of miles and other rewards. United Airlines also offers a refund for tickets that are canceled within 72 hours of the flight's departure date or within 48 hours of check-in, whichever comes first.


United Airlines Un-accompanied minor policy


United Airlines has a policy that requires all unaccompanied minors to be accompanied by an adult. If the minor cannot be accompanied by an adult, they must be placed in the care of United staff or a responsible parent or guardian. If the unaccompanied minor is not placed with United staff or a responsible parent or guardian, they will be transported to their final destination with an accompanying law enforcement officer.
