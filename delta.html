Delta Airlines


Delta Airlines is one of the most well-known airlines in the world. It has a reputation for being one of the best value carriers, with fares that are often lower than those of other major airlines. Delta Airlines also has some of the most innovative features when it comes to airport travel, including its unique Sky Club lounges. Delta Airlines was founded in 1971 and is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia. The airline operates over 560 flights daily to more than 100 destinations in North America, Central America, South America, Europe, Asia Pacific, and Africa. Delta Airlines is a member of the SkyTeam alliance.


History of Delta Airlines


Delta Airlines is an American airline with its headquarters in Atlanta, Georgia. It was founded in December 1929 as a subsidiary of Delta Air Lines, Inc. The airline operates over 5,000 flights per day to 1,900 destinations in more than 50 countries. Delta is the world's largest carrier by passenger traffic and cargo volume.


Delta Airlines began as a small regional airline in 1929. The airline started with two planes and operated routes between Atlanta and Chattanooga, Tennessee. In 1930, Delta Air Lines established a code-sharing agreement with Western Air Express and added services to Memphis, Tennessee and Memphis International Airport. In 1933, Delta became the first airline to operate jet aircraft commercially. The company grew rapidly throughout the 1930s and 1940s due to its strong partnerships with major hotel chains and car companies.


In 1961, Delta opened its first international terminal at John F Kennedy International Airport (JFK). The following year, Delta introduced reservation-based pricing which allowed customers to track their flight status online for the first time. In 1967, Delta merged with Northwest Orient Airways creating the world's largest carrier by fleet size at the time. In 1978, Delta launched its first direct route from Los Angeles to Tokyo Narita International Airport which made it the only U.S.-based carrier to offer nonstop service between Japan and North America.


In 1985, Delta became one of the founding members of Star Alliance which helped increase international traffic volumes by linking together member airlines with common routing systems and airport codes. In 1991, Delta purchased Northwest Airlines which gave the airline a strong presence in the western United States. The following year, Delta became the first airline to operate a fully flatbed aircraft on its mainline fleet. In 1999, Delta acquired Global Air Lines which gave the airline a significant presence in Latin America.


In 2001, Delta merged with American Airlines creating the world's largest airline by revenue. The merger created more than 25,000 new jobs and made Delta the second-largest carrier in the world by passenger traffic. In 2003, Delta launched a global campaign called "Delta One" which aimed to increase customer satisfaction ratings and attract new customers to the airline. In 2006, Delta became one of the founding members of SkyTeam which created a global alliance of airlines that share common flight routing systems, airport codes, and miles.


In 2011, Delta announced plans to purchase Virgin America for $2 billion which would create the world's largest airline by size. The acquisition was completed in 2013 and resulted in approximately 1,500 jobs being eliminated as part of the merger. In 2014, Delta became one of the founding members of LATAM Airlines Alliance which creates a global alliance of airlines that share common marketing, sales, service, and cargo operations.

Delta Airlines Cancellation Policy


When you book a flight on Delta Airlines, there are a few things you should know about their cancellation policy. First of all, Delta does not charge for cancellations that are made within 24 hours of the original departure date. However, if you cancel your flight after this time period has passed, Delta will charge you a $150 fee. Second, Delta offers a travel insurance plan that covers up to $500 per person in case of unexpected travel delays or cancellations. If you have purchased this plan, and your flight is canceled due to weather conditions outside of your control, Delta will rebook you onto the next available flight without any additional fees. Finally, if your flight is canceled due to mechanical issues with the aircraft, Delta will refund your ticket minus the cost of any food and beverage that was ordered.


Delta Airlines Un-accompanied minor policy


Delta Airlines has a policy in place that requires all unaccompanied minors to have travel documents that prove their age. These documents can include a passport, driver's license, or birth certificate. Delta also requires that the unaccompanied minor is under the care of an adult who is accompanying them on the flight.

Delta Airlines Seat Assignments


For years, Delta Airlines used a method of seat assignment that divided passengers into two classes - first and economy. The first class was typically more expensive and afforded passengers more amenities, such as priority boarding and better seats.


However, starting in 2015, Delta began to phase out its first-class seating entirely in favor of a new system called Select Comfort. Under this new policy, all passengers are placed into one of three classes - Select Comfort, Main Cabin Extra, or Standard Class. Select Comfort accommodations are some of the most comfortable on the market, but they come at a price. Main Cabin Extra seats are slightly less comfortable but still offer many of the same benefits as First Class seats did before. Standard Class is the cheapest option and offers reduced amenities and no privileges.


Many people were upset with Delta's decision to discontinue First Class seating, believing that it made the airline less prestigious. Others argue that it was simply time for Delta to change its seating policy and make things more customer-friendly. Whichever side you take on this issue, it's clear that Delta Airlines has faced some tough criticism in recent years for its Seat Assignments.
